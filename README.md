📊 Customer Retention & Loyalty Signal Analysis — Canada Vapes
About the Project
This capstone project was completed as part of the MGMT-6219 Data Analytics Capstone course through a Riipen industry partnership with Canada Vapes, a Canadian e-commerce retailer specializing in vaping products and accessories.
Canada Vapes was experiencing strong customer acquisition but faced a critical retention challenge — maintaining approximately 3,500 returning customers despite a steady inflow of new buyers. The goal of this project was to analyze early customer purchase behavior, identify behavioral signals of loyalty and churn, and develop data-driven retention strategies to increase returning customers to 5,000.
---
My Role

Project Highlights
Retention Driver Identified: Customers making higher-value, multi-item first purchases were significantly more likely to return, while low-value, single-item buyers had the highest churn risk.
Revenue Concentration Discovered: A small proportion of high-value transactions contributed disproportionately to total revenue, revealing a skewed sales distribution.
Top Product Leverage: Demand was concentrated among a limited number of high-performing products — these were recommended as retention anchors for onboarding and cross-selling strategies.
Time-Sensitive Engagement: Analysis showed that early post-purchase engagement (within 7–14 days) is critical for converting first-time buyers into returning customers.
Actionable Strategies Delivered: Recommended product bundling, tiered discounts, automated follow-up campaigns, and targeted loyalty programs — all directly tied to data findings.
